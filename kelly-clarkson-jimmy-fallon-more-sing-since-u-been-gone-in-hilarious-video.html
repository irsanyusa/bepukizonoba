Kelly Clarkson’s 2004 hit, “Since U Been Gone” won a Grammy for Best Female Pop Vocal Performance. It is also notoriously known for being the hardest karaoke song to sing in front of your friends. The notes are seriously so high. Jimmy Fallon enlisted a roster of celebrities to cover the tune with Clarkson during The Tonight Show in 2019.

Can Other Celebrities Hit the High Notes in Kelly Clarkson’s ‘Since U Been Gone’?

Clarkson, Fallon, Shaquille O’Neal, John Oliver, Rachel Brosnahan, Mumford & Sons, Meghan Trainor, Anthony Anderson, and The Roots all performed vocal gymnastics during the song. Clarkson started off singing in her signature lower register. O’Neal licked his lips as he took a stab at the second line. He was a rapper back in the day but is not so much of a singer now.

Fallon strummed an air guitar and we didn’t expect anything less from the very animated host. The highlight of the song was Oliver singing “yes, yes” instead of “yeah, yeah.” Trainor really brought it home with her incredible vocal abilities. Since this video was posted, fans have been asking her to release a full cover of “Since U Been Gone.”

Oliver starting screaming during the hook. Those high notes are no joke. Anderson’s version of singing basically sounded more like motivational speaking. Everyone launched into air guitar solos. The members of Mumford & Sons threw in some awkward dance moves.

This impromptu music video resembles a scene from the 2012 movie Pitch Perfect where those trying out for a capella groups must sing “Since U Been Gone.” Just as you imagined, the high notes in the hook proved to be quite the challenge for the singers. Only Ben Platt and Ester Dean were able to nail the song perfectly.

Clarkson’s Grammy for “Since U Been Gone” is obviously very well deserved. The American Idol winner is constantly pushing herself vocally. She even hits some unreal notes in her Kellyoke covers on The Kelly Clarkson Show. She is one of the best singers in the music world without question.
