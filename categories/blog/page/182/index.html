Los Angeles Earthquake Rattles Residents

published on July 09, 2024
March 17, 2014 9:57 AM EDT A 4.4-magnitude earthquake rattled the Los Angeles metropolitan area on Monday morning. The epicenter of the quake was located just north of the city’s Beverly Hills neighborhood, near Westwood, and seven miles north of Santa Monica, according to data from the U.S. Geological Survey. It struck at 6:25 a.m. local time. Residents of Los Angeles reported feeling the earthquake, though the Los Angeles Fire Department tweeted that it had no immediate reports of damage or injury.
