The Best Ways to Learn Czech While Studying in the Czech Republic - Live &amp; Study

16. 4. 2023 | Study in the Czech Republic

Studying in a foreign country can be a challenging experience, especially if the language is unfamiliar. For international students studying in the Czech Republic, learning Czech is an excellent way to fully immerse themselves in the culture and connect with locals. Here are some of the best ways to learn Czech while studying in the Czech Republic.

  • Enroll in Czech language courses at your university

    • Most universities in the Czech Republic offer Czech language courses for international students. These courses are designed to help students develop their language skills and gain a better understanding of the culture. They are often offered at different levels, from beginner to advanced, and may be included in the tuition fees. Taking these courses is an excellent way to start learning Czech, meet new people, and gain confidence in your communication skills.

  • Practice with locals

    • One of the best ways to learn Czech is to practice speaking with locals. This can be done by joining conversation groups, attending cultural events, or simply striking up a conversation with someone at a café. The more you practice speaking with others, the more comfortable you will become with the language. Additionally, locals can provide insight into the nuances of the language and culture that may not be covered in language courses.

  • Watch Czech movies and TV shows

    • Watching movies and TV shows in Czech is an excellent way to improve your listening skills and get a better understanding of the language. You can start with Czech movies with English subtitles and work your way up to Czech movies without subtitles. Watching Czech TV shows can also be a great way to learn the language while enjoying local entertainment.

  • Read Czech literature

    • Reading Czech literature can be an excellent way to improve your reading and comprehension skills. Start with children’s books and work your way up to more complex literature. Reading can also help you learn new vocabulary and grammar rules in context.

  • Use language learning apps

    • Language learning apps like Duolingo, Babbel, and Rosetta Stone can be great resources for learning Czech. These apps offer interactive lessons that focus on building vocabulary, grammar, and conversation skills. Many also include pronunciation exercises and progress tracking features.

    In conclusion, learning Czech while studying in the Czech Republic can be a fun and rewarding experience. By enrolling in Czech language courses, practicing with locals, watching Czech movies and TV shows, reading Czech literature, and using language learning apps, international students can improve their language skills and gain a deeper understanding of the culture.
